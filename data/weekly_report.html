中文摘要整理
研究目的 本隨機對照試驗旨在探討ed to explore the efficacy of combining high‑flux hemodialysis (HFHD) with a hypoxia‑inducible factor prolyl hydroxylase inhibitor (HIF-PHI) for the treatment of renal anemia among patients with end-stage kidney disease.
研究方法 Sixty-four newly treated maintenance hemodialysis (MHD) patients were allocated to 4 groups (n = 16 each): low-flux hemodialysis (LFHD) plus recombinant human erythropoietin (rhEPO), LFHD plus HIF-PHI, HFHD plus rhEPO, and HFHD plus HIF‑PHI.
主要結果 All groups showed increases in HB and RBC post‑treatment (P <.05). The increases in hemoglobin (ΔHB) and RBC (ΔRBC) were significantly greater in the HFHD + HIF-PHI and LFHD + HIF-PHI groups than in the LFHD + rhEPO and HFHD + rhEPO groups.
結論 Compared to low-flux dialysis plus rhEPO, the combination of HFHD and HIF-PHI shows more favorable improvements in hemoglobin levels, iron metabolism markers, and clearance of middle-molecule toxins in patients with incident MHD.
